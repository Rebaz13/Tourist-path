About Kurdistan

information in general

Kurdistan literally translates to "Land of the Kurds." It is referred to as the Kurdistan Region in the Iraqi Constitution. The Kurdistan Regional Government is the official name of the government (often abbreviated as the KRG). The Kurdistan Regional Government (KRG) is sometimes known as Kurdistana Bashur or Bashuri Kurdistan (South Kurdistan), due to its geographical location within the wider Kurdistan area. Iraqi Kurdistan, often known as the Kurdistan Area, is a self-governing region in Iraq. It is bordered on the east by Iran, on the north by Turkey, on the west by Syria, and on the south by the remainder of Iraq. Erbil, commonly known as Hawler, is the regional capital. The Kurdistan Regional Government is in charge of the region..

History
  • Some key events since the early 20th century.

Some key events since the early 20th century. 1918: Sheikh Mahmoud Barzinji becomes governor of Sulaimaniya under British rule. He and other Kurdish leaders who want Kurdistan to be ruled independently of Baghdad rebel against the British. He is defeated a year later.

GEOGRAPHY
  • Area: 40,643 km²
  • Population: 3,757,058

The Kurdistan Region comprises parts of the three governorates of Erbil, Slemani and Duhok. It borders Syria to the West (Rojava), Iran to the East (Rojhalat), and Turkey (Bakur) lying to the North where fertile plains meet the Zagros Mountains.
